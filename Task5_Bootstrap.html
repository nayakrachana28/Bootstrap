LushGreen Adenium Plant

Adenium, also called the Desert Rose, strikes a mid-point between quintessential outdoor plants and indoor flowering plants. They bloom in hues of red, pink, white and yellow and can be planted in regular-sized pots. If you have direct sunlight in your home during summers, the Desert Rose will fit right in because it needs to be exposed to 6 hours or more of sunlight.

Climbing Vines

Climbing vines help direct the eye up to encompass many architectural details in the landscape. They also may produce flowers, fruit, attract butterflies or pollinators, feed birds, shade an area or simply cover up a failing fence or other structure. Most of the vines for zone 9 are tough and need little extra care outside of watering and training them to a trellis or arbor.

FRUIT PLANTS

In a botanical sense, a fruit is the fleshy or dry ripened ovary of a flowering plant, enclosing the seed or seeds. Apricots, bananas, and grapes, as well as bean pods, corn grains, tomatoes, cucumbers, and (in their shells) acorns and almonds, are all technically fruits.
